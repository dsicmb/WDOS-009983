A
LITTLE bit of information about this LITTLE
wild cat


The smallest cat in the world, about the size of a six month old domestic cat, the rusty, like its name suggests, has short brownish-grey fur, overlaid by a rufous hue – though in Sri Lanka, it has a more russet colour. The cat’s face has two dark streaks that run along each cheek and four stripes extending above its eyes, back between the ears and then down along the shoulders, eventually tapering off to elongated spots. These elongated spots can also be seen along its flanks as very faint rusty-brown spots and blotches. Its throat, chest and belly are stark white, and marked with large black spots and bars, much like that of a leopard. It has small rounded ears, black soled feet, and a tail that is just about half its body length.

After all, who wouldn't fall in love with a 1 kg rusty-spotted cat, so tiny it can fit in the palm of your hand? ... Whilst owning these cats is not illegal, like dog-wolf hybrids, these mongrels are not tame as they still have their wild side present and it is safe to say that they don't make good pets either.

OUR DEAREST VISITOR , YOU , WELCOME TO DEHIWALA ZOO

It is our utmost pleasure to welcome YOU to one of the oldest yet exclusive Zoos in Asia , The Dehiwala National Zoo, which has an extraordinary collection of exotic and indigenous fauna.

Quoting “ OLD IS GOLD ” , our zoo, nick named as COLOMBO ZOO , dwells 72 species of Mammals , 65 species of Birds, 31 species of Reptiles, 89 species of Fish, 03 families of Amphibians and 30 species of Butterflies & many more that is not quite easy to be listed . The total number of animals varies from a whopping 2500-3000 due to breeding and free living colonies of birds. Dehiwala Zoo has become quite an attraction among the visitors not only for the exclusive collection of local and exotic animals but also for landscaping with lush greeneries with orchids ,calatheas ,anglonemas & many more .

The Zoo supports and promotes conservation by breeding certain rare and endangered species through proper animal welfare.Annually, over one and half million local and foreign tourists visit the Zoo. and over 2000 students and groups of students from schools and universities visit the Zoo for educational purposes.

The Zoo is taking an extensive effort to maintain an enhanced pace with the changing world which in turn enlightening its standards. It has been involved in some major redevelopment programmes with the aim of enhancing the overall image of the Zoo while providing maximum facilities for animals and YOU , our dear visitors.

Our Key Responsibilities ...

  1. To maintain a diversified native and exotic collection of animals in near natural habitats.
  2. To assure the survival, well-being and natural behavioral characteristics captive animals.
  3. To ensure ex-situ conservation of wild fauna species with special emphasis to threatened, endangered and endemic species of the country.
  4. To preserve the animals that is on the brink of extinction.
  5. To conduct conservation breeding programs.
  6. To conduct research to widen the knowledge base on animals and on other zoological disciplines.
  7. To exhibit captive animals with minimal disturbance to their lifestyle for knowledge gain and amusement.
  8. To conduct education and outreach programs to assist informal education especially of school children.
  9. To cater to the tourism industry through innovative concepts of display and by promoting ecotourism.
  10. To formulate policies, plans and guidelines for administration and management of the zoological establishments and aquariums in the country.
  11. To provide safe and satisfying environment to workers and visitors.
  12. To collate and participate actively in international associations and networks of zoological gardens and animal exchange programs.
  13. To encourage zoo based special interest organizations, foreign and local volunteers, external researchers, students and groups of interested parties to work with the department.
  14. To develop supportive facilities such as feed farms, audio visual units, equipped auditoriums, modern laboratories, animal hospitals, separation units, museums, libraries, etc.
  15. To adhere to proper environmental safety measures.
  16. To operate uninterrupted maintenance and upgrading programs.
  17. To launch mutually beneficial  management programs with general public and schools.

Our Orchid Arcade simulates the experience of ascending through a tropical rainforest and showcases a diversity of orchids from the rich low to high-elevation rainforests around the world . Each of the display houses within the Orchid Archade has been expanded beyond their original size, increasing by almost threefold the combined display area.

We preserved part of the original frame of the original cool house and curated the orchid collection in the new Arcade according to five biogeographical regions – Malesia, Continental Southeast Asia and South Asia, Australasia, Afrotropics and the Neotropics. Researchers will be able to study rare orchids that are naturally found only at higher elevations, such as such as Cymbidiums (also known as Boat Orchids),Dendrochilums (also known as Chain Orchids), Cyrtochilums and Phragmipediums (also known as Slipper Orchids). This will greatly benefit taxonomic and floristic research, as researchers will be able to grow rare species, before identifying and documenting them at a later stage when they flower.

Our colourful Parrot Paradise , the rainbow universe , takes you on a trip to South America & Australia to discover ecosystems as rich as they are fragile. Through the immersive routes of the rainforests of amazon you will meet delightful macaws & parrots , often threatened with extinction in their natural habitats are safe & sound under their new home as our island itself is a tropical paradise which is enriched enough for them to thrive under our protection.

Parrot Paradise works hand in hand with the Parrot Wildlife Foundation, an organization that participates in the conservation and reproduction of endangered psittacidae, as well as all species present in their natural habitats. Thanks to the expertise of caretakers and veterinarians, some species present in the park are also the subject of breeding programs, overseen by the European Association of Zoos and Aquariums, in order to avoid their disappearance if they were to occur. extinguish in the wild.

For visitors Policies related to Public Health and COVID-19, Click Here



  • Visitor safety :
    Rails, moats, rocks, fencing and other barriers between you and the animals are there to protect both you and the animals from harm. Please do not lift children over rails, let them play on rocks, rest them on railings or stick fingers or arms through fences. Do not enter non-public areas.


  • Smoking :
    To provide a healthy, safe environment for visitors, staff and animals, the zoo prohibited smoking of any type, including vaping and electronic cigarettes.


  • Pets :
    Service animals are permitted. Please call ahead if you plan to bring a service animal. Please do not leave pets in your vehicle.


  • Feeding Animals :
    All zoo animals are on a carefully structured diet, and introducing outside food can harm them. So unless otherwise you want to feed certain zoo animals with the food provided by the zoo keepers and our staff during the specified feeding times , please refrain from feeding our beloved animals with harmful outside food.


  • Respecting Animals :
    Please show respect for the zoo's inhabitants by refraining from banging on windows, throwing objects into exhibits, harassing or teasing the animals. Show them love and compassion just like us they also feel pain , joy , anger and sadness . So please be empathetic.


The Dehiwala Zoo reserves the right to exclude anyone who engages in inappropriate or offensive conduct, as determined by zoo staff. The zoo is not responsible for lost, stolen or damaged articles and is not required to issue refunds or exchanges for inclement weather.
